# Crates Product Bible

**Version:** 0.1.0
**Status:** Living Document
**Owner:** Cody Hart

---

# Mission

**Crates exists to deepen the relationship between people and the music they physically own.**

We're not building software to catalog records.

We're building software that helps people reconnect with their collections.

---

# Vision

Crates is the home for your physical music collection.

It is a place to organize, remember, and rediscover the albums, CDs, cassettes, and other physical media that matter most.

The application begins as a vinyl-first experience because vinyl collectors are underserved by modern software. Over time, Crates will expand to support additional physical music formats while maintaining the same thoughtful experience.

Our goal is not to replace Discogs.

Our goal is to become the application people open after they've identified the record they want.

---

# Why We Exist

Physical music has become increasingly meaningful.

Collectors don't simply own albums.

They own stories.

Every copy has a history.

Where it came from.

Who gave it to them.

When it was played.

How it shaped their musical journey.

Most software treats these objects as inventory.

Crates treats them as part of a person's life.

---

# The North Star

> **Every feature should make it easier—or more enjoyable—to reconnect with the music you already own.**

Whenever we're uncertain about a feature or product decision, this question should guide us:

**Does this deepen the relationship between the user and their collection?**

If not, it probably doesn't belong in Crates.

---

# Product Pillars

Everything we build should strengthen at least one of these pillars.

## Organize

Help people organize their collection in ways that feel natural.

Not just by artist or genre, but by mood, purpose, memories, and personal workflows.

Organization should be flexible without becoming complicated.

---

## Remember

Every physical copy has a story.

Crates should help users preserve those stories through journals, memories, notes, ratings, and listening history.

Software should help memories last longer.

---

## Rediscover

The greatest value of a collection isn't owning music.

It's returning to music you forgot you loved.

Crates should constantly encourage rediscovery through thoughtful design, not interruption.

---

# Product Philosophy

Collections tell stories.

Ownership matters.

Artwork deserves to be seen.

Metadata should support the experience—not dominate it.

Software should quietly get out of the way.

Beautiful products invite exploration.

The best organization feels effortless.

Power users should never come at the expense of approachability.

---

# Design Philosophy

Crates should feel:

- Calm
- Warm
- Thoughtful
- Personal
- Premium
- Timeless

It should never feel:

- Clinical
- Cluttered
- Overwhelming
- Overly technical
- Database-driven

Album artwork is the visual hero.

Typography establishes hierarchy.

Metadata is progressively disclosed instead of presented all at once.

Motion should reinforce navigation and context—not exist for decoration.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

We optimize for maintainability over cleverness.

Documentation drives implementation.

Offline functionality is a core product feature.

Architecture should support future growth without adding unnecessary complexity today.

Build for today's users while leaving thoughtful extension points for tomorrow.

Every major architectural decision should be documented.

---

## Primary

---

## Secondary

Music lovers who want a beautiful way to organize and revisit their collection.

---

## Long-Term

Power users including DJs, archivists, and audiophiles.

# What Makes Crates Different

Discogs is the world's best music database.

Spotify is an incredible music streaming experience.

Crates is neither.

Crates is the home for your collection.

It focuses on ownership rather than access.

It emphasizes relationships rather than metadata.

It values personal context as much as catalog information.

---

# What We Are Not

Crates is not:

- A marketplace
- A streaming platform
- A social network
- An inventory management system
- A replacement for Discogs
- A clone of Spotify

These may influence our thinking, but they do not define our product.

---

# Principles for Every Feature

Before implementing any feature, ask:

1. Does it help organize a collection?
2. Does it help preserve memories?
3. Does it encourage rediscovery?
4. Does it feel approachable?
5. Does it support offline use?
6. Is it consistent with the rest of the product?
7. Would someone still appreciate this feature five years from now?

If the answer to most of these questions is "no," reconsider the feature.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Over time, Crates should become the definitive companion for physical music collectors.

Future capabilities may include:

- Intelligent recommendations
- Smart crates
- Collection valuation
- Rich track metadata
- AI-assisted discovery
- DJ preparation tools
- Additional physical media formats
- Community features

These ideas are intentionally deferred until the core experience is exceptional.

# Building Philosophy

We are not trying to build the biggest collection app.

We are trying to build the one people enjoy opening the most.

Every decision should favor quality over quantity.

Every interaction should reinforce the joy of owning physical music.

The goal is not to manage collections.

The goal is to help people spend more time listening to the music they love.
